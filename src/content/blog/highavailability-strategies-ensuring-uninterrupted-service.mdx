# Introduction

High-availability (HA) is the ability of a system to remain operational and accessible even in the event of a failure or outage.

HA is critical for organizations that provide mission-critical services, such as e-commerce, banking, healthcare, and more. When a system fails, it can lead to loss of revenue, damage to reputation, and even legal issues. Hence, it is essential to have a robust high-availability strategy in place.

In this article, we will discuss various HA strategies, their advantages and disadvantages, and how to choose the right strategy for your organization.

# Active-Passive

In an active-passive HA strategy, there are two identical systems, one active and one passive.

> The active system serves all requests, while the passive system remains idle, constantly syncing with the active system.

If the active system fails, the passive system takes over the workload, ensuring uninterrupted service. This strategy is relatively easy to implement, as it requires minimal changes to the existing infrastructure.

# Active-Active

> In an active-active HA strategy, there are multiple identical systems that share the workload.

Each system serves a portion of the requests, and all systems remain active simultaneously.

This strategy provides better scalability and performance than the active-passive strategy. However, it also requires more complex infrastructure and load-balancing mechanisms.

# N+1

In an N+1 HA strategy, there are N active systems and one spare (N+1).

The spare system remains idle, ready to take over the workload of any failed system.

This strategy provides excellent redundancy and fault-tolerance. However, it also requires more resources and is more complex to implement than the other two strategies.

# Conclusion

High-availability is crucial for any organization that provides mission-critical services. Choosing the right HA strategy depends on various factors such as cost, complexity, scalability, and performance requirements.

Active-passive, active-active, and N+1 are some of the commonly used HA strategies, each with its own advantages and disadvantages. By understanding these strategies, organizations can design and implement a robust HA solution that ensures uninterrupted service and meets their business needs.
